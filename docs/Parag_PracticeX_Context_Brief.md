# Practice X and Parag Context Brief

## Purpose

This document summarizes the Practice X conversation, product situation, partnership considerations, and current strategic interpretation regarding Parag. It is intended to be used as context in a future Computer session or as a reference before continuing negotiations.

## Current Situation

Practice X started as a discussion around agreement, lease, and contract tracking, originally connected to a broader idea of using AI agents to monitor rent, lease rates, renewal obligations, and contract terms. The concept later evolved into a healthcare-focused product for independent and group medical practices.

The current positioning is:

> Practice X is the command center for the business side of independent medical practices.

The product is meant to centralize and organize the non-patient-care side of a medical or surgical practice so physicians can focus on patient care, while administrators can focus on revenue opportunities, payer contract negotiations, compliance, renewals, and operational follow-through.

Practice X has already moved beyond a concept. Within approximately four days, a functioning mock/real product and public-facing website were created at [PracticeX](https://practicex.ai/). The product direction includes command center workflows, contracts, renewals, obligations, alerts, document upload, review queues, facility-level views, and healthcare-specific modules such as HIPAA, OSHA, employee tracking, and contract intelligence.

## How the Product Evolved

The idea initially touched commercial lease/agreement management, including franchise or restaurant-style use cases. Parag then identified multiple healthcare use cases based on his own practice environment and operational pain.

The product direction now includes:

- Risk review of clauses and obligations.

- Centralized meeting repository.
- OSHA and HIPAA administrative tracking.

- Potential future integrations with HR systems such as ADP.
- No EHR integration required in Phase 1.

The strongest product framing is:

> Practice X helps independent and group medical practices centralize contracts, compliance, reimbursement intelligence, administrative meetings, renewals, and operational tasks in one command center, without requiring an enterprise IT project.

## Target Customer

The best initial customer segment is:

> Independent multi-location specialty practices, GI groups, ASCs, and other medical/surgical practices that are too complex for spreadsheets but too cost-conscious or fast-moving for enterprise hospital systems.

This segment is attractive because:

- They have real administrative complexity.
- They often manage multiple locations.
- They deal with payer contracts, leases, vendor agreements, employee documents, and compliance requirements.

- They are motivated to stay independent.
- They are sensitive to payer economics and administrative inefficiency.
- They may not require EHR integration for the first product wedge.
