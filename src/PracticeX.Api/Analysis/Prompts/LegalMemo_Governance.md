## Corporate Governance — Issue Overlay

This document is a board resolution, action by written consent,
founders'/board meeting minutes, or operating-rules artifact. Run this
overlay in addition to the master register.

### Authority and validity
- **Quorum present** — was the meeting (or unanimous written consent)
  attended/signed by the required number of directors / shareholders?
  Quote the bylaws/op-agreement quorum threshold and verify against
  the document.
- **Voting threshold** — was the action approved by the threshold
  required by the bylaws / op-agreement / DGCL §141 / state statute
  (e.g. simple majority, supermajority for certain actions)?
- **Notice or waiver of notice** — meetings without proper notice are
  defective unless attendees waived notice in writing or by attendance
  without objection. Flag if neither appears.
- **Conflicts of interest** — DGCL §144 (interested-director
  transactions) requires disclosure + disinterested approval OR fairness
  to the corporation. Surface any director with a conflict and verify
  the procedural protections were observed.
- **Authority of the body** — was this an action the board could take
  alone, or did it require shareholder approval (charter amendment,
  M&A, dissolution, increase in authorized shares)?

### Action types — common landmines

- **Stock issuance** — purchase price set; FMV / 409A basis cited;
  stockholder consent if exceeding authorized shares; preemptive
  rights respected; ROFR / co-sale notices given.
- **Option grant approval** — strike price = FMV per 409A; grant date
  = approval date (not back-dated; **back-dating is a securities-law
  and tax problem**); vesting commencement clearly stated.
- **Officer / director election or removal** — D&O insurance still in
  effect; indemnification agreement signed (not just authorized);
  resignation letters for departing.
- **Compensation** — independent committee or §144 procedure followed;
  FMV / commercially-reasonable analysis documented (especially for
  related-party comp).
- **Banking resolution** — signing authority specifically enumerated;
  expiration / revocation mechanics; consistent with operating-doc
  authority limits.
- **M&A / asset purchase** — fairness opinion (where required); shareholder
  approval for sale of "all or substantially all" assets; appraisal-
  rights notice; HSR pre-merger filing; Section 280G analysis if
  parachute payments triggered.
- **Charter / bylaw amendment** — proper supermajority; certificate
  of amendment filed with state; effective date.
- **Dissolution / wind-down** — solvency representation; creditor
  notice; tax clearance; final tax returns; section 332 analysis
  for tax-free liquidation if applicable.
- **Adoption of equity plan** — within 12 months of stockholder approval
  (Rule 701 / ISO requirement); stockholder approval document referenced.

### Procedural defenses
- **Signature pages** — every required signatory present? unanimous
  written consent must be signed by **all** directors (DGCL §141(f)).
- **Date** — date of action vs date of signature vs effective date —
  any drift?
- **Counterparts clause** — for multi-signer documents.
- **Filing obligations** — does this action require a state filing
  (charter amendment, foreign qualification update)? If yes, is there
  evidence of filing?

### D&O and indemnification
- **Indemnification of directors and officers** — does the board
  resolution invoke a previously-authorized policy? is there an
  indemnification agreement on file?
- **Advancement of expenses** — pre-judgment vs post-judgment; secrecy
  vs disclosure; clawback if found not entitled.
- **D&O insurance maintenance** — was the policy renewed; does the
  resolution increase exposure beyond policy limits?

### Fiduciary duty and corporate-opportunity
- **Disclosure of opportunities** — when a director / officer takes a
  business opportunity that could belong to the company, the corporate-
  opportunity doctrine requires disclosure and rejection by the board
  before the individual proceeds. Flag if document evidences a
  corporate-opportunity scenario without proper procedure.
- **Self-dealing** — interested-party transactions properly disclosed?
- **Duty of oversight (Caremark)** — does the document evidence
  systematic monitoring of legal/regulatory compliance, or is it silent
  in a way that erodes Caremark protection?

### Practical landmines for early-stage companies
- **Founders'-meeting minutes that read like a casual chat** — omit
  required formalities (notice, quorum, votes recorded). Flag and
  recommend an "omnibus ratification" resolution to cure historical
  defects in one pass.
- **Missing state-of-incorporation filings** — charter amendments approved
  but never filed at the state.
- **No registered-agent confirmation** — agent change resolutions that
  weren't followed up with state filings.
- **Stale director list** — minutes refer to directors who already
  resigned; verify current board composition matches.
- **Equity issuances without 409A** — option grants approved without
  reference to a current 409A valuation; flag the §409A risk.
