## NDA / Confidentiality Agreement — Issue Overlay

NDAs are deceptively simple. The asymmetries hide in the definitions and
the carve-outs. Run through this overlay in addition to the master register.

### Direction and scope
- **Mutual vs one-way?** A "mutual" NDA where only one party will share
  meaningful information is effectively one-way. Read the actual flows.
- **Definition of "Confidential Information"** — does it require marking?
  oral disclosures + later confirmation? does it pick up "the existence
  of discussions" itself? does it pick up "Evaluation Material" derived
  from CI (residuals)?
- **Permitted purpose** — narrow ("evaluating a potential commercial
  relationship") vs broad ("any business purpose"). Narrower protects
  the receiving party against scope-creep claims; broader serves the
  disclosing party.

### Standard carve-outs (these should always exist)
- Already known to the receiving party
- Independently developed without use of CI
- Lawfully received from a third party
- Compelled by legal process (with notice and cooperation provisions)

If any of these are *missing*, that is a material gap.

### Term and survival
- **Confidentiality term** — 2-year, 3-year, 5-year, perpetual; trade
  secrets typically perpetual under state UTSA, ordinary CI is a
  negotiated period.
- **Discussion period vs confidentiality period** — they are different
  clocks. The discussion period gates *new* disclosures; the
  confidentiality period gates how long received CI must be protected.
- **Survival of obligations after termination** — return / destroy
  language; certification; system-backup carve-out.

### Hidden teeth
- **Non-solicit / no-poach** — a "we won't hire each other's employees"
  clause is increasingly hidden in M&A NDAs. Flag scope (employees vs
  contractors), duration, and post-termination tail.
- **Standstill provisions** — in M&A NDAs, the receiving party agrees
  not to acquire securities of, or make a tender offer for, the
  disclosing party for X years. Hugely consequential if this NDA is
  about evaluating us as an acquisition target.
- **Non-circumvent** — "you will not contact our customers / vendors
  directly to bypass us" is sometimes drafted as a non-compete in
  disguise.
- **Exclusivity / no-shop** — a clause forbidding the discloser from
  talking to other counterparties about the same opportunity for X
  months. This is a deal-economics provision dressed as confidentiality.
- **Residuals clause** — "nothing prevents the receiving party's
  personnel from using general knowledge retained in unaided memory" —
  typically pushed by tech companies, weakens CI protection significantly.

### Equitable remedies
- **Injunctive relief without bond** — common but worth flagging; without
  it, breach litigation is purely damages-based, which is often a
  toothless remedy for confidentiality breaches.
- **Liquidated damages** — uncommon in NDAs; if present, scrutinize.

### Compliance and disclosure
- **Permitted disclosees** — typical: employees, advisors, with need-to-
  know AND a confidentiality obligation at least as strict. Watch for
  disclosees that include "potential financing sources" or "potential
  acquirers" — that's broader than it looks.
- **Process for compelled disclosure** — notice to the disclosing party
  + cooperation to seek a protective order; without this, a subpoena
  is a free pass.
- **Government investigations** — increasingly important; a clause
  permitting disclosure to securities regulators / DOJ without notice is
  increasingly mandated by SEC whistleblower rules and is *not* a
  carve-out the disclosing party can resist.

### Strategic reading
- **Who initiated this?** A counterparty NDA template (their paper)
  usually favors the discloser; reverse if it's our paper.
- **Acquirer signal** — if the recitals reference "evaluating a strategic
  transaction," "potential acquisition," "merger discussions," this is
  M&A intelligence — flag in §5 (Material Disclosures) for board
  awareness.
- **Recruiting signal** — if recitals reference "evaluating personnel"
  or non-solicit goes both ways, this may be lateral-recruitment
  intelligence.
- **Health system / PE signal** — counterparty class matters; flag
  whether this is a hospital system, a private-equity firm, a competing
  practice, or a vendor.
