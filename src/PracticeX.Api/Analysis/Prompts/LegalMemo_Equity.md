## Equity / Financing Instrument — Issue Overlay

This document is an equity grant, equity plan, term sheet, SAFE, or
convertible note. The economics are usually straightforward; the
asymmetries hide in the protective provisions, transfer restrictions,
and conversion mechanics. Run this overlay in addition to the master
register.

### If this is a term sheet or SAFE / convertible note

- **Valuation** — pre-money vs post-money; for SAFEs the post-money
  variant (YC 2018+) shifts dilution risk to founders vs the pre-money
  variant. Quote the cap and any discount.
- **Discount rate** — typical 15–25%; stacked discount + cap is
  uncommon and worth flagging.
- **Most-favored-nation (MFN)** — pulls the SAFE/note holder up to any
  better terms granted to a later investor; carve-outs (different
  instrument types? trigger a re-papering?).
- **Pro-rata rights** — major-investor threshold; sunset on next
  qualified financing; assignment to affiliates.
- **Conversion mechanics** — what triggers conversion (qualified
  financing dollar threshold), what happens on liquidity event,
  treatment in a dissolution.
- **Information rights** — what financials are owed and at what cadence;
  major-investor floor.
- **Voting / consent rights** — protective provisions (key actions
  requiring SAFE/note-holder approval); board composition;
  observer rights.
- **No-shop / exclusivity** — duration; carve-outs for fiduciary out;
  break-up fee.
- **Standstill** — investor's commitment not to acquire securities
  outside the deal; duration.

### If this is an equity grant (founder stock, option, RSU, restricted stock)

- **Vesting schedule** — duration (typically 4 years); cliff (typically
  1 year); ratable thereafter (monthly vs quarterly); back-dating to
  service start vs grant date.
- **Acceleration** — single-trigger vs double-trigger on change of
  control; what counts as "good reason"; partial vs full acceleration;
  how the formula works (% of remaining unvested? or accelerated
  monthly?).
- **Early exercise** — permitted? right of repurchase at exercise price
  for unvested shares? requires 83(b) within 30 days (CRITICAL — flag
  if the grant is restricted stock and the 30-day window is at risk).
- **Repurchase rights** — company's right to repurchase unvested or
  forfeited shares; price (cost vs FMV vs lesser of); window after
  termination.
- **Right of first refusal (ROFR)** — company's ROFR on transfers;
  carve-out for permitted transfers (estate planning, affiliates).
- **Co-sale / drag-along** — typical in venture deals; surface scope
  and trigger threshold.
- **Lock-up / market standoff** — IPO lock-up obligation (typically
  180 days); applies to all securities or just newly-issued?
- **Transfer restrictions** — board approval required? class voting?
  insider trading window?
- **Tax treatment** — for options: ISO vs NSO; ISO $100K limit; AMT
  trap on early exercise. For restricted stock: 83(b) election window
  (30 days from issuance, hard deadline). For RSU: tax timing on
  vesting. **The 83(b) miss is a six-figure tax mistake** — flag
  prominently if the document is restricted stock and the 30-day
  window is unclear or expired.
- **Strike price** — for options: 409A valuation basis; date of
  valuation report; risk if the grant is older than the 12-month 409A
  window.
- **Exercise period post-termination** — typical 90 days for ISO
  preservation; longer windows (extended exercise, post-termination
  cashless) increasingly common but disqualify ISO treatment.

### If this is an equity plan / 2024 Equity Incentive Plan

- **Pool size** — # of shares reserved; % of fully-diluted; evergreen
  refresh provision.
- **Plan administrator** — board vs compensation committee.
- **Permitted award types** — ISO, NSO, RSU, restricted stock, SAR,
  performance shares.
- **Eligible recipients** — employees only? include directors,
  consultants?
- **Repurchase right defaults** — what the company gets if the plan
  document is silent and the individual award document doesn't
  specify.
- **Change-of-control treatment** — default acceleration provisions
  for awards under the plan; ability to override in individual award
  agreements.
- **Section 162(m) compliance** (if public-company-bound) — performance
  metric pre-approval, individual award caps.

### Reps and warranties / standard provisions
- **Securities-law representations** — accredited investor status;
  Regulation D / 506(b) vs 506(c); resale restrictions and Rule 144
  legend.
- **Investment representation** — investment intent (not for
  distribution); no public market reliance.
- **No general solicitation** — important for 506(b) deals; flag if
  the document references public marketing.
- **State-law qualifications** — California 25102(o), other state blue-
  sky compliance.

### Common landmines
- **83(b) windows missed** — see above; CRITICAL severity.
- **Acceleration silence** — if the grant document is silent on CIC
  acceleration, the founder/employee gets no acceleration; flag.
- **ISO disqualification** — exercise period >90 days post-termination
  silently converts ISO to NSO with ordinary-income tax treatment.
- **Vesting commencement date drift** — written grant says one date,
  service began on another, equity plan says yet another — surface the
  ambiguity and recommend a vesting confirmation letter.
- **Implicit dilution from MFN + later instrument differences** — a
  SAFE with MFN that later sees a convertible note with a lower cap
  triggers MFN; this gets missed by founders.
- **Stacked SAFEs without clear cap-table impact** — model the post-
  money cap-table assuming worst-case conversion; multiple uncapped
  post-money SAFEs can give away more equity than founders realize.
