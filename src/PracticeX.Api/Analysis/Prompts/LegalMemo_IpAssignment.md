## IP Assignment / PIIA — Issue Overlay

This document is a Proprietary Information and Inventions Agreement,
Confidential Information and Invention Assignment, contractor IP
assignment, or similar artifact. PIIA gaps are the most common
deal-killing finding in M&A diligence on early-stage companies. Run
this overlay in addition to the master register.

### Scope of assignment
- **What's assigned** — "all inventions, original works of authorship,
  developments, concepts, improvements, designs, discoveries, ideas,
  trademarks or trade secrets" — the broad-form. Flag if narrower.
- **Made when** — during employment / engagement; at company facilities;
  using company resources; relating to company business; relating to
  research or anticipated research. The strongest forms cover all of
  these; weaker forms cover only "during employment" or "using company
  resources."
- **Present-tense automatic assignment** — "I hereby assign" (present
  tense) is enforceable in California per *Stanford v. Roche*; "I will
  assign" (future tense) requires a separate assignment document.
  **This is a CRITICAL distinction** for any company with material
  IP — a future-tense PIIA leaves the company without title until the
  individual signs again.
- **Work made for hire** — copyrightable works are work-for-hire only
  for "employees within the scope of employment" or specific 1976 Act
  categories for contractors; for any other contractor work, an explicit
  assignment is required (and the present-tense rule above applies).
- **Moral rights waiver** — civil-law jurisdictions and California's
  artist-resale statutes preserve moral rights; explicit waiver
  preferred where enforceable.

### Pre-existing IP carve-out
- **Schedule of prior inventions** — is there a schedule attached?
  is it filled in or blank? a blank schedule is a finding ("no prior
  inventions") that may or may not be true.
- **Background IP scope** — what does the assignor own coming in vs
  what gets assigned out — surface the boundary.
- **License back of background IP** — if assignor brings in IP that
  the company will need to use, is there a non-exclusive license back?
  Without it, the company can be held up by an ex-employee asserting
  background-IP claims.

### State-law statutory carve-outs

These supersede contractual scope. The PIIA must reference (or at minimum
be consistent with) them, and is unenforceable to the extent it tries
to assign rights the statute reserves to the employee:

- **California Labor Code §2870** — employee retains rights to
  inventions developed entirely on own time without using company
  resources, EXCEPT inventions relating to company's business or
  research, OR inventions resulting from employee's work for the
  company. Statutory notice required (§2872).
- **Delaware §805 (Title 19)** — similar carve-out; statutory notice.
- **Illinois 765 ILCS 1060/2** — similar.
- **Kansas K.S.A. §44-130** — similar.
- **Minnesota §181.78** — similar.
- **North Carolina N.C.G.S. §66-57.1** — similar.
- **Utah §34-39-3** — similar.
- **Washington RCW 49.44.140** — similar.

If the PIIA does not reference the applicable statute or include the
required statutory notice, the carve-out still applies by operation of
law, but the document is procedurally non-compliant — flag.

### Confidentiality and post-employment obligations
- **Confidential information scope** — definition; carve-outs (already
  known, independently developed, lawfully received, public domain).
- **Post-employment confidentiality term** — perpetual for trade secrets
  (typical), defined period for ordinary CI.
- **Return / destruction obligation** — at termination; certification.
- **Non-disclosure of third-party CI** — extends company's protections
  to CI received under NDA from customers / partners; common in
  enterprise-software companies.

### Restrictive covenants (often bundled)
- **Non-compete** — increasingly unenforceable; California voids
  outright (Bus & Prof §16600); FTC's 2024 nationwide ban was vacated
  but is being relitigated; jurisdiction matters enormously.
- **Customer non-solicit** — typically more enforceable than non-
  compete.
- **Employee non-solicit / no-poach** — DOJ has criminally enforced
  no-poach agreements between employers since 2016; flag if scope is
  broad.

### Federally-mandated whistleblower protections
- **Defend Trade Secrets Act notice (18 USC §1833)** — required
  notice to employees that trade-secret claims do not bar reporting
  violations of law to the government. **Without this notice, the
  company cannot recover exemplary damages or attorneys' fees** in a
  trade-secret case. Flag as MEDIUM if missing.
- **SEC whistleblower preemption** — confidentiality clauses cannot
  prohibit reporting securities violations to the SEC; recent SEC
  enforcement actions ($60M+ fines) for clauses that did. Carve-out
  required.

### Common deal-killing findings in M&A diligence
- **Founder PIIA missing** — no signed PIIA from a founder is the
  single most-common diligence finding. The company may not own its
  core IP. Recommend an immediate "confirmatory IP assignment" from
  every founder.
- **Contractor IP assignment missing** — code contributed by a
  contractor without a PIIA may not be company-owned. Flag every
  significant contributor and recommend back-papering.
- **Future-tense assignment language** — see above; recommend
  re-papering with present-tense language.
- **No DTSA notice** — see above; recommend updating PIIA template +
  ratification by current employees.
- **Schedule of prior inventions blank or missing** — flag; recommend
  affirmative confirmation that there are no prior inventions, or list.
- **Non-employee personnel signing employee PIIA** — different statutory
  regime; the document may not assign as intended.
