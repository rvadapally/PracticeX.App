## Employment / Executive Agreement — Issue Overlay

Run through this overlay in addition to the master register. For physician
employment specifically, the healthcare-compliance section is mandatory.

### Compensation
- **Base salary** — stated annual; payment frequency.
- **Productivity / RVU model** — formula clarity; floor and ceiling;
  reset cadence; data-source ownership; audit rights.
- **Bonus structure** — discretionary vs formulaic; clawback triggers;
  pro-ration on partial-year departures.
- **Equity / partnership track** — vesting (cliff + ratable); single-
  trigger vs double-trigger acceleration on change of control; transfer
  restrictions; ROFR; valuation methodology on departure (book value
  vs FMV vs formula); buyout terms (lump sum vs note over years vs life
  insurance funding).
- **Signing bonuses / loan forgiveness** — repayment trigger language;
  pro-ration; dispute over "for cause" termination.
- **Tail insurance** — practice-paid? employee-paid? silent? — this is a
  five-figure to six-figure obligation that practices routinely ignore
  until departure. **Silence is a HIGH-severity finding.**

### Term and termination
- **Initial term** — and renewal mechanics; auto-renewal traps for
  the employee.
- **Termination for cause** — what is "cause"? specific enumerated list
  vs vague catch-all? notice and cure period?
- **Termination without cause** — notice period; severance; conditions
  on severance (release, non-disparagement, post-termination cooperation,
  full satisfaction of restrictive covenants).
- **Termination for good reason** (employee's quit-with-severance trigger)
  — duty changes? compensation reductions? relocation thresholds?
- **Death / disability** — disability standard (own-occupation vs any-
  occupation, length); buyout treatment.

### Restrictive covenants
- **Non-compete** — duration (12, 18, 24 months); geographic radius
  (specific miles, specific counties, specific zip codes); scope (any
  medicine vs the specialty); blue-pencil clause; tolling on breach.
  **Enforceability varies dramatically by state** — California voids
  most non-competes; FTC's 2024 non-compete rule was vacated in court
  but remains contested; surface jurisdiction.
- **Non-solicit of patients** — stronger than non-compete in most
  jurisdictions; watch for definitions of "patient" (treated within X
  years).
- **Non-solicit of employees** — duration and scope.
- **Confidentiality / trade secret** — typically perpetual.
- **Liquidated damages for breach of restrictive covenant** — uncommon
  but enforceable in some jurisdictions if reasonable.
- **Injunctive relief** — practice's right to enjoin without bond.

### Healthcare-specific compliance
- **Stark / Anti-Kickback** — if employee refers to ancillary services
  (imaging, lab, ASC, pharmacy) owned by the practice, compensation
  arrangement must fit a Stark exception (typically the bona-fide
  employment exception at 42 USC §1395nn(e)(2)). Compensation cannot
  vary based on volume or value of referrals to the ancillary.
- **FMV / commercial reasonableness certification** — explicit language
  in the recitals or comp section that compensation is FMV and was
  determined without regard to referrals — this is the audit defense.
- **Federal program participation** — representation that physician is
  not excluded from Medicare/Medicaid; ongoing notification obligation.
- **Medical staff and credentialing** — obligation to maintain
  credentials at named hospitals; consequences of credentialing loss.
- **Malpractice insurance** — claims-made vs occurrence; tail (see
  above); limits.
- **Quality metrics / value-based care** — increasingly tied to
  compensation; data ownership / dispute rights critical.

### Personal liability and indemnification
- **Practice indemnity of physician** — for acts within scope of
  employment? carve-outs (gross negligence, intentional misconduct,
  fraud, criminal acts)?
- **Physician indemnity of practice** — uncommon but appears; flag.
- **D&O coverage** — if physician is also officer / director, separate
  indemnification agreement and D&O insurance designation.

### IP and work product
- **Work-for-hire / IP assignment** — assignment of all inventions,
  patents, copyrights to practice (subject to state-law carve-outs in
  CA, MN, IL etc. for inventions developed on personal time without
  practice resources).
- **Pre-existing IP schedule** — list of IP physician brings in.
- **Publishing rights** — physician's right to publish research; review
  process; practice's review window.

### Change of control (practice's CIC)
- **Acceleration of vesting / equity** — single-trigger vs double-trigger.
- **Right to terminate without prejudice** — physician's right to walk if
  practice is sold (especially relevant if sold to PE or hospital system).
- **Ongoing obligations** — do restrictive covenants survive a CIC?

### Amendment-specific (if this is an addendum)
- **What is the addendum changing?** Quote prior and new language.
- **Cumulative effect** — does the chain of addenda materially alter
  the original economic deal?
- **Ratification of prior** — silent terms in the master agreement are
  ratified by an amendment that doesn't touch them.
