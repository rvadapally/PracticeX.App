## Generic Corporate Contract — Issue Overlay

This document didn't match a known family template. Run the master issue
register, then add domain-specific scrutiny based on what kind of
agreement you determine this to be. Identify the agreement type in your
posture snapshot (§1) before working through this overlay.

### If this is a commercial agreement (MSA, services, SOW, vendor)
- **SLA mechanics** — service-level metrics, measurement methodology,
  service credits (capped at what %?), termination right after N
  consecutive months of SLA failure.
- **Acceptance criteria** — for deliverables; deemed acceptance after
  N days; rejection process and cure right.
- **Data rights** — who owns the data? data-portability obligations
  on termination; deletion certification; backup retention.
- **Service descriptions and "deemed scope"** — does the SOW pull in
  out-of-scope work via "and any other services reasonably necessary"?
- **Pricing and payment** — fee structure; right to audit; price-
  increase mechanics (cap on annual increases? notice period?); late-
  payment interest and termination right.
- **Subprocessors / subcontractors** — vendor's right to use; notice
  obligations; flow-down requirements.

### If this is a corporate-governance document (bylaws, op agreement, shareholder agreement)
- **Voting thresholds** — supermajority for what? simple-majority for
  what? do these match the company's strategic posture (founder vs
  investor-favorable)?
- **Board composition** — number of seats, who appoints, founder seats,
  investor seats, independent seats; protective provisions / negative
  covenants requiring board or class consent.
- **Transfer restrictions** — ROFR, ROFO, drag-along, tag-along,
  permitted transfers (affiliates, estate planning).
- **Anti-dilution** — full-ratchet, broad-based weighted-average,
  narrow-based weighted-average; carve-outs (employee plan, M&A,
  strategic).
- **Liquidation preference** — 1x non-participating, 1x participating,
  capped participating; seniority of preferences.
- **Information rights** — financial reporting cadence; inspection
  rights; observer rights.
- **Indemnification of directors and officers** — to fullest extent
  permitted by law; advancement of expenses; D&O insurance maintenance
  covenant.
- **Fiduciary-duty waivers / corporate-opportunity waivers** — only
  enforceable in some jurisdictions (Delaware permits broad waivers
  in LLC operating agreements; others narrower).
- **Dispute resolution** — internal escalation; arbitration; jurisdictional
  carve-outs for injunctive relief.

### If this is a financing document (term sheet, SAFE, convertible note, SPA)
- **Valuation cap / discount** — for SAFEs / notes; conversion mechanics.
- **Most-favored-nation** — for notes / SAFEs; carve-outs.
- **Pro-rata rights** — major-investor threshold; sunset.
- **Information rights** — minimum threshold to receive financials.
- **Voting agreement** — drag-along trigger; coordinated-vote provisions.
- **Investor rights agreement** — registration rights (demand, S-3,
  piggyback); transfer restrictions; co-sale; ROFR.
- **Use of proceeds** — restrictions; reporting.
- **Reps and warranties of the company** — knowledge qualifiers; survival.
- **Closing conditions** — favorable / unfavorable; risk of failure.

### If this is a payment-processor / merchant agreement
- **Reserve / hold-back** — right to impose; size; release schedule.
- **Chargeback liability** — full pass-through to the merchant; caps?
- **Termination rights and exit fees** — early termination fee; minimum
  monthly fees.
- **Rate increases** — notice; right to terminate without penalty if
  rates increase.

### If this is a data / privacy / processor agreement (DPA, BAA)
- **Subprocessor flow-down** — obligation on processor.
- **Breach notification** — hours to notify (24 / 48 / 72); regulator
  notification responsibility.
- **Data location and residency** — restrictions; cross-border transfer
  mechanism (SCCs, adequacy).
- **Audit rights** — frequency; notice; cost allocation; SOC 2 in lieu of.
- **Deletion / return** — on termination; certification.
- **Sub-processors' liability** — direct vs cap-passed-through.

### If this is a corporate transaction (asset purchase, stock purchase, merger)
- **Reps and warranties** — bring-down at closing; knowledge qualifiers;
  materiality scrapes; sandbagging vs anti-sandbagging.
- **Indemnification** — survival period; baskets (deductible vs tipping);
  caps (% of purchase price); fraud carve-out; specific indemnities for
  pre-closing taxes, environmental, ERISA.
- **Escrow / holdback** — % held back; release schedule.
- **MAC clause** — definition; carve-outs (industry-wide, pandemic,
  forecast misses); thresholds.
- **No-shop / fiduciary out** — breakup fees (size, triggers).
- **Closing conditions** — regulatory approvals; consents; HSR.
- **Tax treatment** — taxable vs reorganization; allocation of purchase
  price.

If the document doesn't fit any of these classes, name what it IS in §1
and apply the master register only.
