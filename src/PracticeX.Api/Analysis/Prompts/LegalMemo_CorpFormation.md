## Corporate Formation — Issue Overlay

This document is a certificate of incorporation, articles of organization,
EIN confirmation, foreign-qualification certificate, registered-agent
appointment, franchise-tax statement, or similar formation artifact.
Formation defects rarely surface day-to-day but are deal-killers in
M&A diligence and can void corporate protections in litigation. Run
this overlay in addition to the master register.

### Charter / certificate of incorporation
- **Authorized share count** — number authorized vs number outstanding;
  enough headroom for option pool + contemplated financing? Watch for
  "we used to have 10M authorized, then we issued 9.9M, now we need to
  issue more" — requires a charter amendment + stockholder approval.
- **Par value** — par value vs no-par; impact on Delaware franchise
  tax (the Authorized Shares Method vs Assumed Par Value Capital
  Method — the latter often saves 10x for early-stage companies; flag
  if the company is paying the maximum on the wrong method).
- **Multiple classes / series** — common, preferred, founder common
  with high-vote rights? — surface the structure and any class voting
  thresholds.
- **Indemnification** — does the charter contain a §102(b)(7)
  exculpation clause for directors? without it, directors face
  personal liability for breach of duty of care. Modern charters
  typically extend exculpation to officers (post-DGCL 2022 amendment);
  flag if the charter is older and hasn't been refreshed.
- **Forum selection** — Delaware-incorporated companies typically
  designate the Delaware Court of Chancery as exclusive forum for
  corporate-governance disputes; absence is increasingly unusual.
- **Federal-forum selection for 1933 Act claims** — *Salzberg v. Sciabacucchi*
  blessed these clauses; without one, companies face state-court
  Securities Act claims with no removal right.
- **Effective date** — date of incorporation; matches state filing
  date?
- **Registered agent + registered office** — current? a stale registered
  agent leads to default judgments because the company never receives
  service of process.
- **Incorporator signature** — present and dated.

### EIN and federal tax registration
- **EIN issuance letter** — Letter 147C or original CP 575A; matches
  the entity name and address?
- **Tax election** — for LLCs: default partnership taxation, vs §761
  disregarded, vs §7701 check-the-box election to corporate taxation.
  For corporations: C-corp default vs Subchapter S election (Form 2553
  filed within 75 days of incorporation OR by March 15 of the desired
  effective year).
- **State tax registration** — separate from federal; required in each
  state where the company has nexus.

### Foreign qualification (operating outside state of incorporation)
- **States where the company does business** — has the company
  registered as a foreign entity in each? "Doing business" thresholds
  vary by state; common triggers: physical office, employees, local
  bank account, regular sales activity.
- **Statement of information / annual report** — most states require
  annual or biennial filings; failure to file → administrative
  dissolution → loss of liability shield.
- **California §17707.13 / §6210** — California's particularly
  aggressive about foreign-qualification enforcement (suspension,
  loss of standing to sue).
- **Texas franchise tax certificate** — Texas requires a "certificate
  of account status" for many corporate transactions.

### Franchise tax / annual report compliance
- **Delaware franchise tax** — due March 1 each year for corporations;
  June 1 for LLCs. Late penalties + interest.
- **State of operation annual reports** — varying due dates; multiple
  states often missed by early-stage companies.
- **Method of calculation** — Delaware's Authorized Shares Method
  defaults to a high tax for high-share-count companies; Assumed Par
  Value Capital Method usually lower. **Switching methods can save
  $50K–$150K/year** for early-stage companies; flag if the company is
  paying ASM with high authorized share count.

### Operating agreement (LLC) / bylaws (corporation)
- **Adoption** — bylaws / op-agreement signed by all directors / members?
- **Manager-managed vs member-managed** (LLC) — clearly designated?
- **Board structure** (corporation) — number of directors fixed vs
  range; classification (staggered vs annually elected); removal
  procedure.
- **Officer titles + authority limits** — president, secretary, treasurer
  required (DGCL); CEO is permissive; signing authority for contracts
  above what threshold?
- **Indemnification provisions** — repeated in bylaws + standalone
  indemnification agreements; D&O coverage maintenance covenant.
- **Action by written consent** — permitted? unanimous required for
  some actions.
- **Annual meeting** — required by DGCL §211 (annual stockholder
  meeting); failure → any stockholder can compel the meeting via
  Court of Chancery (DGCL §211(c)).

### Common landmines
- **Authorized shares too low for next financing** — recommend amending
  before the round closes; charter amendment requires stockholder vote
  + state filing.
- **Missing 83(b) elections** for founders — see equity overlay; if
  founder restricted stock was issued at formation without 83(b), this
  is a six-figure tax problem.
- **Section 1202 QSBS holding period** — for C-corps, founder stock
  held >5 years can qualify for federal capital-gains exclusion up to
  $10M / 10x basis. Each restructuring (S-to-C conversion, share
  exchange) can reset the holding period. Flag if the document
  evidences a restructuring without QSBS analysis.
- **Stale officer / director list** — bylaws may name people who long
  since departed; recommend a confirmatory officer-and-director
  certificate.
- **Foreign qualification gaps** — most-common diligence finding;
  recommend a 50-state survey.
- **Annual report lapses → administrative dissolution → liability shield
  pierced** — for any state where filings are out of date, recommend
  immediate reinstatement.
