## Call Coverage Agreement — Issue Overlay

Call coverage agreements (one practice or hospital pays another to cover
on-call duties for a service line) live at the intersection of
healthcare-compliance and operational scheduling. Run this overlay in
addition to the master register.

### Economics
- **Per-day / per-shift rate** — stated dollars; differential for
  weekends, holidays, after-hours.
- **Restricted vs unrestricted call** — restricted (must be on premises)
  commands higher rates than unrestricted; verify the rate matches the
  level of restriction.
- **Activation / response time** — minutes to respond; minutes to be on
  premises; consequences of missing.
- **Procedural billing** — who bills for procedures performed during
  call (the covering physician / practice or the contracting hospital)?
  This is the single biggest economic question — getting it wrong shifts
  six- to seven-figure annual revenue.
- **Lost-revenue offset** — is the per-diem call rate net of, or in
  addition to, the covering practice's right to bill its professional
  fees?

### Compliance — the core issue
- **Stark exception used** — call coverage payments to a referring
  physician must fit a Stark exception. Most common: personal services
  arrangement exception (42 CFR §411.357(d)) — requires written, signed,
  >1 year term, FMV compensation, set in advance, commercially reasonable
  even absent referrals, and not based on volume/value of referrals.
- **AKS safe harbor** — Personal Services and Management Contracts safe
  harbor at 42 CFR §1001.952(d) — overlapping but not identical
  requirements; compliance with both is the goal.
- **FMV documentation** — a third-party FMV opinion is the gold standard.
  Surface presence/absence.
- **Commercially reasonable absent referrals** — recital or attestation
  language; otherwise a vulnerability.
- **EMTALA implications** — hospitals have non-delegable EMTALA duties;
  the call agreement should clarify that the contracting hospital
  retains EMTALA responsibility while the covering physician provides
  professional services.

### Scope and rotation
- **Service lines covered** — specific specialty / sub-specialty.
- **Days / shifts covered** — calendar specificity; right of first
  refusal to cover incremental shifts.
- **Backup obligations** — who covers if the on-call physician is
  unavailable? acceptable substitutes (qualifications)?
- **Mandatory minimums** — practice's commitment to staff X shifts/month.

### Quality and clinical
- **Clinical protocols / privileges** — physician must have admitting
  privileges at the contracting hospital; consequences of loss of
  privileges.
- **Quality metrics** — response time, mortality, readmission, NPS;
  reporting and dispute rights.
- **Peer review and credentialing** — physician's submission to hospital's
  peer-review process; due-process protections; HCQIA reporting risk.
- **Medical record obligations** — documentation standards; access.

### Liability and insurance
- **Professional liability** — physician's malpractice coverage; named
  insured / additional insured arrangements; aggregate limits adequate
  for hospital-volume call.
- **Tail coverage** — who pays on termination? this routinely costs
  $50K-$250K+ for a physician switching mid-career.
- **Indemnification** — mutual? carve-outs?
- **EMTALA penalty allocation** — if the hospital takes an EMTALA
  penalty for a call-coverage failure, who pays?

### Term and termination
- **Initial term** — Stark requires ≥1 year; verify.
- **Renewal** — auto-renew vs negotiated; rate-reset mechanism.
- **Termination for cause** — specific list; notice and cure.
- **Termination for convenience** — both sides? notice period
  appropriate (90 days is typical; 30 is short for a physician's
  scheduling ramifications); Stark prohibits multiple short-term
  agreements that aggregate to >1 year — flag.
- **Tail insurance trigger** — termination triggers tail purchase by
  whom?

### Operational and scheduling integration
- **Schedule publishing cadence** — how far in advance must the schedule
  be published?
- **Swap rights** — can the practice trade shifts with another covering
  group? notice required?
- **Coverage during practice partner's vacation** — explicit?
- **Holiday coverage** — separate rate? mandatory rotation?
